# Cymonic Corporate Expense Policy (India)

**Document Owner:** Finance & Compliance
**Applies To:** All Cymonic employees, contractors, and travelers in India
**Currency:** INR (Rs.)
**Version:** 1.0

---

## Page 1 — Purpose and Scope
1. This policy defines reimbursable expenses for Cymonic employees in India.
2. All expenses must be business-related and reasonable.
3. Reimbursements are limited by category caps specified herein.
4. Submissions must include valid receipts for approval.
5. Non-compliant claims may be rejected or flagged for review.
6. Auditors may override AI verdicts with documented comments.

## Page 2 — Definitions
1. "Claim" means any submitted expense request.
2. "Approver" means a designated auditor or finance reviewer.
3. "Metro city" includes Mumbai, Delhi NCR, Bengaluru, Hyderabad, Chennai, Pune, Kolkata.
4. "Standard city" means any non-metro location.
5. "Business purpose" describes the business justification for the expense.
6. "Receipt" means itemized proof of purchase.

## Page 3 — General Rules
1. All expenses must be pre-approved where noted.
2. Receipts are mandatory for all expenses above Rs. 500.
3. Duplicate claims are prohibited.
4. Claims must be submitted within 30 days of the expense date.
5. Exceptions require written approval from a Director or above.
6. Personal expenses are not reimbursable.

## Page 4 — Expense Categories Overview
1. Meals and incidentals
2. Travel and transport
3. Lodging
4. Client entertainment
5. Office supplies and equipment
6. Training and certifications
7. Telecom and internet
8. Miscellaneous and special cases

## Page 5 — Breakfast Policy
1. Breakfast cap: Rs. 250 per person per day.
2. Claim must be itemized.
3. Alcohol is not reimbursable.
4. Only business travel days are eligible.
5. Room service breakfast is allowed within cap.
6. Tips must be reasonable (<= 5%).

## Page 6 — Lunch Policy
1. Lunch cap: Rs. 400 per person per day.
2. Client lunches require attendee details.
3. Split bills must be clearly documented.
4. Alcohol is not reimbursable.
5. Non-itemized receipts will be flagged.
6. Home-delivery is allowed for on-site work.

## Page 7 — Dinner Policy
1. Dinner cap: Rs. 600 per person per day.
2. Metro city cap: Rs. 750 per person per day.
3. Client dinners require business purpose details.
4. Single-person "team building" meals are not allowed.
5. Alcohol is not reimbursable.
6. Tips must be reasonable (<= 5%).

## Page 8 — Snacks and Tea
1. Snacks/Tea cap: Rs. 150 per person per day.
2. Must be connected to extended work sessions or travel.
3. Receipt required above Rs. 200.
4. Alcohol is not reimbursable.
5. Multiple snack claims in one day may be flagged.

## Page 9 — Daily Meals Cap
1. Combined meals cap: Rs. 1,500 per person per day.
2. Includes breakfast, lunch, dinner, and snacks.
3. Exceeding the cap requires prior approval.
4. For metro cities, cap is Rs. 1,800 with approval.

## Page 10 — Local Transport (Taxis/Rideshare)
1. Per-trip cap: Rs. 300 in standard cities.
2. Metro cap: Rs. 400 per trip.
3. Premium or luxury rides are not reimbursable.
4. Receipts required for each ride.
5. Airport transfers allowed with receipt.

## Page 11 — Public Transit
1. Metro, bus, and train fares are reimbursable.
2. Receipts required when available.
3. Monthly passes need manager approval.
4. First-class tickets are not reimbursable.
5. Intercity travel follows travel policy.

## Page 12 — Mileage (Personal Vehicle)
1. Mileage rate: Rs. 10 per km.
2. Only for approved business travel.
3. Distance must be logged with route details.
4. Parking and tolls are reimbursable with receipts.
5. Insurance and maintenance are not reimbursable.

## Page 13 — Parking and Tolls
1. Parking reimbursable when business travel is required.
2. Receipts required for all parking fees.
3. Toll receipts required for reimbursement.
4. Premium parking is discouraged and may be flagged.

## Page 14 — Air Travel (Domestic)
1. Economy class only.
2. Book at least 7 days in advance when possible.
3. Changes must be justified.
4. Excess baggage requires approval.
5. Priority boarding is not reimbursable.

## Page 15 — Air Travel (International)
1. Economy class only unless approved by VP.
2. International travel must be pre-approved.
3. Visa fees are reimbursable with receipts.
4. Travel insurance requires approval.
5. Lounge access is not reimbursable.

## Page 16 — Rail Travel
1. AC 2-tier or equivalent recommended.
2. Premium classes require approval.
3. Rail meals must follow meal caps.
4. Receipts required for reimbursement.

## Page 17 — Lodging (Standard Cities)
1. Nightly cap: Rs. 3,000.
2. Hotel stays require manager approval.
3. Taxes are reimbursable within cap.
4. Mini-bar, movies, and personal charges are not reimbursable.

## Page 18 — Lodging (Metro Cities)
1. Nightly cap: Rs. 4,500.
2. Exceeding cap requires Director approval.
3. Long-stay discounts should be requested.
4. Complimentary breakfast does not increase meal cap.

## Page 19 — Long-Term Stays
1. Stays over 7 nights require special approval.
2. Serviced apartments may be preferred.
3. Laundry reimbursement limited to Rs. 500/week.
4. Local travel during long stays follows normal caps.

## Page 20 — Client Entertainment
1. Requires detailed attendee list.
2. Must include business purpose.
3. Alcohol is not reimbursable.
4. Total entertainment cap: Rs. 2,500 per event.
5. Non-itemized receipts will be rejected.

## Page 21 — Team Events
1. Must be approved by HR or Manager.
2. Cap: Rs. 1,000 per person.
3. Alcohol is not reimbursable.
4. Event date and purpose must be documented.

## Page 22 — Office Supplies
1. Standard supplies reimbursable with receipts.
2. Cap: Rs. 1,500 per month.
3. Non-business items are not reimbursable.
4. Bulk purchases require approval.

## Page 23 — Equipment Purchases
1. Requires pre-approval from IT/Manager.
2. Cap: Rs. 10,000 per item.
3. Warranty and service contracts require approval.
4. Personal use is prohibited.

## Page 24 — Telecom and Internet
1. Mobile reimbursement: Rs. 800/month.
2. Home internet reimbursement: Rs. 1,000/month with approval.
3. Roaming expenses require pre-approval.
4. Itemized bills required.

## Page 25 — Training and Certifications
1. Requires manager approval.
2. Cap: Rs. 20,000 per year.
3. Receipts and proof of completion required.
4. Exam retakes require approval.

## Page 26 — Conferences and Events
1. Must be pre-approved by Manager.
2. Registration fees reimbursable.
3. Travel and lodging follow standard caps.
4. Premium add-ons are not reimbursable.

## Page 27 — Work From Home Expenses
1. Ergonomic equipment requires approval.
2. Cap: Rs. 2,000 per year.
3. Office chair reimbursement capped at Rs. 6,000 with approval.
4. Utilities are not reimbursable.

## Page 28 — Relocation Expenses
1. Must be pre-approved by HR.
2. Moving services capped at Rs. 20,000.
3. Temporary lodging follows standard caps.
4. Personal travel during relocation is not reimbursable.

## Page 29 — Client Gifts
1. Requires manager approval.
2. Cap: Rs. 1,000 per client per year.
3. Cash or cash equivalents are prohibited.
4. Receipts required.

## Page 30 — Subscription Services
1. Requires manager approval.
2. Cap: Rs. 2,000 per year.
3. Must be directly related to work.
4. Auto-renewal requires review.

## Page 31 — Medical/Health Expenses
1. Not reimbursable unless explicitly approved.
2. Company health insurance applies.
3. Exceptions require HR approval.

## Page 32 — Safety and Security
1. Safety-related expenses may be reimbursed with approval.
2. Examples include emergency transport and safe lodging.
3. Must be documented with incident report.

## Page 33 — Advances
1. Expense advances require Director approval.
2. Advances must be settled within 15 days.
3. Excess advance must be returned immediately.

## Page 34 — Reimbursement Timelines
1. Standard processing time: 7-10 business days.
2. Delays may occur for missing information.
3. Urgent claims require Finance approval.

## Page 35 — Required Documentation
1. Itemized receipts required.
2. Business purpose description mandatory.
3. Attendee list required for client meals.
4. Missing receipts above Rs. 500 are rejected.

## Page 36 — Fraud and Misuse
1. Falsifying claims is a disciplinary offense.
2. Repeated misuse results in loss of privileges.
3. Legal action may be taken for severe violations.

## Page 37 — Audit and Compliance
1. Claims may be audited at any time.
2. AI audit provides preliminary risk status.
3. Auditors make final decision.
4. Audit logs are retained for 3 years.

## Page 38 — Policy Exceptions
1. Exceptions require written approval.
2. Exceptions must include justification.
3. Exceptions are recorded in audit trail.
4. Exceptions expire after the approved period.

## Page 39 — Currency and Exchange
1. INR is the official reimbursement currency.
2. Foreign expenses must be converted at official rates.
3. Exchange fees are reimbursable with receipts.

## Page 40 — Alcohol and Prohibited Items
1. Alcohol is not reimbursable.
2. Personal entertainment is not reimbursable.
3. Fines and penalties are not reimbursable.
4. Gambling expenses are strictly prohibited.

## Page 41 — Approval Authority
1. Employees submit claims.
2. Managers review and approve.
3. Finance team performs final audit.
4. VP approval required for exceptions.

## Page 42 — Policy Updates
1. Policy updates are versioned.
2. Each new policy version must be communicated.
3. Effective date applies to new claims.
4. Historical claims may be re-audited.

## Page 43 — Contact and Support
1. For questions, contact finance@cymonic.example.
2. For emergencies, contact HR.
3. For policy clarifications, contact Compliance.

## Page 44 — Acknowledgment
1. Employees must acknowledge policy changes.
2. Lack of acknowledgment does not exempt compliance.
3. Policy acceptance is required for reimbursements.
